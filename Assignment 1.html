Traditinal food of Jodhpur



 
             1.Dal Baati Churma                  2.Gatte Ki Sabzi                       3.Mirchi Bada                         4.Ker Sangri 
            

                            
         
            

            1. Dal Baati Churma
            

            This famous dish need no introduction. It is the state's classic signature dish. Dal Baati Churma is 
            synonymous to RAJASTHAN ; known for its crunchy Batis , dipped in ghee along with spicy Dal and sweet 
            Churma .The Dal is made of lentils while Churma is a coarsely ground wheat mixture crushed and cooked in ghee
            and jaggery or sugar and served along with cold Chhaach with fresh mint leaves.
            
            
            

            2. Gatte Ki Sabzi
            
 
             Most of Rajasthani cuisine came to fruition as a result of the arid conditions. For This 
             dish you don't need any fresh vegitables and that's its speciality. This curry is made with gram flour dumplings
             (steamed and lightly fried) and tangy gravy made up of tomato, buttermilk and spices . It's best enjoyed with rotis
             or tandoori naan.
            
            
            

            3. Mirchi Bada
            
 
            Another snacky delight from Rajasthan , Mirchi Badas are perfect answers to complement evening tea or 
            morning breakfast. The hot and spicy badas are scrumptiously delicious and served with spicy fried green chillies ,
            tomato sauce or occasionally with mint and tamarind chutney. Jodhpur aae or Choudhary ka Mirchi Bada nhi khaya
            to kya khaya .   
            
            
            

            4. Ker Sangri
            
  
            A delightful bean and berry combo unique to Rajasthan , Ker Sangri is traditional , spicy sabzi
            made with the Ker berry and Sangri bean . Cooked simply and easily with common ingredients like red chillies , 
            carom seeds and a couple of spice powders . Ker Sangri is neverthless extremly tasty, leaving a lingering taste
            on your toungue. The additional of raisins brings out the brilliance of this recipe , as it enhances the flavour
            of all the spicy ingredients while also providing sweet respites in every spoonful.
